# Sample Saudi HR / Labor Compliance Corpus

> This is a synthetic sample corpus for portfolio and evaluation purposes. It is not legal advice and should not be used as an official legal source.

## source_id: saudi_labor_law_sample_article_109

Annual leave: A worker is entitled to annual leave with full wage. The leave duration is commonly modeled in this sample as not less than 21 days per year, increasing to not less than 30 days if the worker spends five consecutive years with the employer.

## source_id: saudi_labor_law_sample_probation

Probation period: A worker may be subject to a probation period if it is clearly stated in the employment contract. This sample treats probation rules as requiring explicit contractual agreement and clear duration.

## source_id: saudi_labor_law_sample_end_of_service

End-of-service award: The sample corpus includes end-of-service concepts such as years of service, wage basis, and termination context. Calculations should be handled carefully and flagged for human/legal review when incomplete data is provided.

## source_id: internal_hr_policy_sample_remote_work

Remote work policy: Internal HR policy may require manager approval, role eligibility, secure access, device compliance, and audit logging for remote-work requests.

## source_id: internal_hr_policy_sample_pii

PII handling: HR assistants must redact national IDs, phone numbers, private emails, employee numbers, and payroll identifiers unless explicitly authorized and necessary.
